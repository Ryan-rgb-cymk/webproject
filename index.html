Calvin and Hobbes

The comic series Calvin and Hobbes by Bill Watterson ran for ten years under the Universal Syndicate, being published in over 2400 newspapers in at least 50 countries. Richly complex and beautifully simple, Watterson's comic plays between imagination and reality, optimism and cinicism, and whimsy and sincerity as it follows proud and exasperating 6-year-old Calvin and his toy tiger Hobbes, who comes to life to support and antagonize him.

Characters

Watterson didn't want to clutter his comic with lots of characters, but rather keep it centered on Calvin and Hobbes and just include a few people immediately around him, each filling a different role.
